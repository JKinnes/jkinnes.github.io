About Trawling

"Bottom trawling is a fishing practice that herds and captures large amounts of specimens to collect a single target species. It is accomplished by towing a large net behind the boat and along the ocean floor, and it is the most popular fishing method used today. Trawling is not only used as a fishing method, but also as a surveying method to collect mass amounts of data from underwater ecosystems."


"Trawling is accomplished by setting floats atop the head rope and trawl opening which create a roof for the fish to be caught beneath. Ships use special gear attached to the footrope to create the opening. The ‘fishing circle’ is created by the head and foot ropes attached to the main ‘sweeps’."

Equipment

  • Sweeps - Large ropes connected to the boat
  • Sinkers - weights put at the bottom of the net openings along the footrope
  • Footrope - the rope that connects the sweeps at the bottom of the mouth of the net
  • Floaters - flotation devices put along the headrope to keep the net opening open
  • Headrope - The top rope of the net opening that connects the sweeps
  • Fishing circle - The netting area which collects the fish
  • Back Square - a deeper part of the net that funnels the collected fish
  • 'HNO' Horizontal Net Opening - the mouth of the net

Pros

"Trawling is a highly efficient method for fishing, and as the most popular method of fishing, it has been used for many years. Trawling is a highly efficient way to collect marine specimens and can easily survey and collect data from marine ecosystems Trawling is the leading method of fishing and is the leading stimulant of the fishing economy "

Cons

"The efficiency of trawling is also its downfall as the large nets destroy ecosystems and capture many bycatch species, heavily raising mortality rates Trawling was a necessary evil when fishing was heavily depended on for survival, however now that human survival isn't dependant on efficient fishing techniques, these drawbacks aren't reasonable Trawl fishing devastates the seafloor, damaging essential flora and fauna "
