Regulations

"Both federal and state regulations define restrictions on trawling across the United States. These regulations set quotas on species taken while trawling, in addition to limiting size, season, gear, and areas where trawling can be used. These rules help manage and preserve fishery resources at both the national and local levels."

Federal

Equipment

  • “It is unlawful for any vessel using large footrope gear to fish for groundfish shoreward of the boundary line approximating the 100 fm (183 m)” (Code of Federal Regulations)
  • “Lines or ropes that run parallel to the footrope may not be augmented with material encircling or tied along their length such that they have a diameter larger than 19 inches”
  • A vessel may not have both groundfish trawl gear and non-groundfish trawl gear onboard simultaneously

Area

Selective flatfish trawl gear is required shoreward of the boundary line approximating the 100 fm (183 m) depth contour between 42° N lat. and 40°10′ N lat. and fishing with all other types of small footrope trawl gear is prohibited in this area.

Species

  • “It is unlawful for any vessel using large footrope gear to fish for groundfish shoreward of the trawl RCA, defined at § 660.11 and with latitude and longitude coordinates at §§ 660.71 through 660.74” (Section C)
  • Selective flatfish trawl gear is required shoreward of the boundary line approximating the 100 fm (183 m) depth contour between 42° N lat. and 40°10′ N lat. and fishing with all other types of small footrope trawl gear is prohibited in this area.
