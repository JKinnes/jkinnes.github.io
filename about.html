Throughout high school, Josh spent most of his time on the North Kingstown Senior High School's robotics team, learning the basics of both engineering anbd programming. In his senior year, he was appointed lead programmer of FIRST Tech Challenge team 6527. This team would go on to place third in the 2016 Rhode Island FIRST Tech Challenge state competition, and secure a spot at the East Super Regional tournament in Scranton, Pennsylvania. This experience was instrumental in helping to set Josh on the path he is pursuing today.

Shortly after graduating high school, Josh enlisted into the Rhode Island Army National Guard as a 15T UH-60 Black Hawk repairer. In 2017, He deployed with 1-126 General Support Aviation Batallion (GSAB) in support of Operations Inherent Resolve and Spartan Shield in both Kuwait and Iraq. In 2020, when the COVID-19 pandemic was at its peak, Josh supported Task Force Guardian within the Rhode Island National Guard to provide aid, care, and immunization support. In early 2022, he attended the Noncomissioned Officer Academy at Ft. Dix, New Jersey and obtained the rank of Sergeant before leaving the Guard in June of that year.

After returning from his deployment in 2018, Josh began to make arrangements to study Advanced Automotive Technology at New Ingland Institute of Technology in fall 2019.He completed 3 terms of study before the COVID-19 pandemic made attending school impossible. During his time there, he gained experience in engine repair, automotive electrical circuits, multiple welding and cutting techniques, automotive service communication, and more. He has used these experiences and credits to gain multiple jobs in the automotive field, and jump start his degree progress at the University of Rhode Island.

In late 2021, Josh made his largest career jump to date. He was hired to work for MIKEL Inc as a logistician on the Naval Undersea Warfare Center in Middletown, Rhode Island. Since there, he has helped the PBL logistics depot to maintain and organize millions of dollars worth of submarine equipment, and was eventually moved to the OHIO Class Test and Evaluation Team. On the T&E team, Josh has been able to utilize his mechanical experience to test and maintain multiple systems for nuclear submarines. His attendance to the University of Rhode Island is in direct support of his goal to rise higher within NUWC, and see what the future brings.
